# Procurement Policy

## Purpose

This policy governs the intake, triage, and approval routing for new vendors and vendor renewals.

## Standard intake requirements

Every new vendor request must include:

- Vendor name
- Requesting team
- Business owner
- Cost center
- Business justification
- Vendor category
- Requested start date
- Annual contract value
- Contract term
- Quote or order form
- Contract excerpt or agreement
- Description of data the vendor will access
- Whether the vendor is new or an existing vendor

## Document requirements by category

### SaaS and software vendors

SaaS and software vendors must provide:

- Quote or order form
- Contract or order-form terms
- Security questionnaire
- Data processing agreement if the vendor processes personal data, customer data, employee data, or confidential business data
- Subprocessor list if the vendor processes personal data or confidential data
- SOC 2 Type II report or equivalent security attestation for medium-risk and high-risk vendors

### Professional services vendors

Professional services vendors must provide:

- Statement of work
- Quote
- Business justification
- Confidentiality terms if they will access internal company data

### Office, facilities, and low-risk operational vendors

Low-risk operational vendors must provide:

- Quote
- Business owner approval
- Tax form or vendor setup form
- Payment details

Security review is not required unless the vendor will access company systems, employee data, customer data, confidential data, or production environments.

## Approval routing

Procurement owns initial triage for all new vendors.

A request cannot be marked ready for approval while required information is missing.

The agent may recommend approval routing, draft follow-up messages, and create internal tickets. The agent must not approve a vendor, commit spend, accept contract terms, or send external communications without human approval.

## Escalation triggers

Escalate to a human procurement owner when:

- Required intake fields are missing
- Documents conflict with each other
- The vendor may already exist under a similar name
- Contract value, data access, or vendor terms trigger cross-functional review
- The request asks to bypass normal approvals
