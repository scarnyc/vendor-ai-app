# Legal Review Policy

## When Legal review is required

Legal review is required when:

- Annual contract value exceeds $50,000
- Total contract value exceeds $100,000
- Contract term exceeds 12 months
- Payment terms exceed Net 60
- The vendor processes personal data, customer data, employee data, or confidential business data
- The vendor uses subprocessors outside the United States
- The contract includes non-standard liability, indemnity, confidentiality, auto-renewal, or termination language
- The contract allows the vendor to use company, customer, or employee data to improve products, train models, or develop benchmarks
- The vendor requests unusual governing law or dispute-resolution terms

## Data protection terms

If a vendor processes personal data or confidential business data, Legal should confirm that:

- A data processing agreement is in place
- The subprocessor list is disclosed
- Breach notification language is acceptable
- Data retention and deletion obligations are documented
- The vendor cannot use company data for model training unless explicitly approved

## Non-standard clauses

The following clauses require Legal review:

- Limitation of liability below 12 months of fees
- Unlimited unilateral vendor termination rights
- Auto-renewal longer than 12 months without notice
- Vendor right to use customer data to improve models or services
- Governing law outside the United States
- Assignment rights without notice

The agent may summarize legal issues and route them for review. The agent must not provide final legal approval.
